Criteria Used in the Clinical Grading Scale for Malignant Hyperthermia

From Larach et al [ 1994 ], Rosenberg et al [ 2015 ]

  • Questions
  • Manifestation
  • Clinical Finding (Maximum Score)
  • Choose
  • 1
  • Manifestation  End-tidal CO2 > 55 mm Hg, PaCO2 > 60 mm Hg
  • Clinical Finding Respiratory acidosis (15)
  • 2
  • Manifestation  Unexplained sinus tachycardia, ventricular tachycardia, or ventricular fibrillation
  • Clinical Finding Cardiac involvement (3)
  • 3
  • Manifestation  Base deficit > 8 mEq/L, pH < 7.25
  • Clinical Finding Metabolic acidosis (10)
  • 4
  • Manifestation  Generalized rigidity, severe masseter muscle rigidity
  • Clinical Finding Muscle rigidity (15)
  • 5
  • Manifestation  Serum creatine kinase concentration > 20,000/L units, cola-colored urine, excess myoglobin in urine or serum, plasma [K+] > 6 mEq/L
  • Clinical Finding Muscle breakdown (15)
  • 6
  • Manifestation  Rapidly increasing temperature, T > 38.8° C
  • Clinical Finding Temperature increase (15)
  • 7
  • Manifestation  Rapid reversal of MH signs with dantrolene ( score=5 )
  • Clinical Finding Other
  • Manifestation  Elevated resting serum creatine kinase concentration ( score=10 )
  • Clinical Finding Other
  • 8
  • Manifestation  Consistent with autosomal dominant inheritance
  • Clinical Finding Family history (15)
